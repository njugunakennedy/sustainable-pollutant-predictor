Objectives

1.2. Research Objectives
- To fabricate biochar from milk sludge for the
adsorption of organic matter (OM) and nutrients from
milk processing wastewater.
- Conduct batch adsorption experiments to
evaluate the performance of the biochar in removing
pollutants from MPWW and to characterize the biochar
properties.
- Propose directions for the application of
biochar in treating multiple pollutants via simulation,
thereby enhancing the sustainability of wastewater
treatment in the dairy sector.

Scope 

3.2 Two Tank Set-up Methodology

To simulate an industrial scale of milk fat COD and
anionic removal, the Brookside milk plant in Kenya was
selected as a case study. The plant has a daily production
of 500,000 liters of milk, with the main products being
skim milk, butter, ghee, and cheese [10]. By following
their daily volumes of both wastewater and sludge, a
system design of two tanks in series was developed.
Based on data from the United Nations Food and
Agriculture Organization, milk processing produces
2400 kg of wastewater per ton of milk processed. Sludge
produced is about sludge production is about 0.5 kg per
kg of removed COD in aerobic (mostly used in Kenya)
and in anaerobic systems about 0.1 kg per kg of removed
COD [11]. This translates to 1,236,000 liters of milk
wastewater per day and 6,144 kg of sludge per day. The
biomass yield was 3.17 g per 10 g; thus, 6144 kg of
sludge will yield 1947.65 kg, and the aim is to reduce
milk fat COD and anionic pollutants.
